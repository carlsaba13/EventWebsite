The Biggest Concert of 2022
BTS
The worldwide K-pop sensation brings their latest releases to the stage of Street Sosh. BTS is the best selling artist in South Korean history, and over the years, their success and impact have extended beyond sales. BTS' numerous accolades include six American Music Awards, nine Billboard Music Awards, 24 Golden Disk Awards, and nominations for two Grammy Awards and two Brit Awards.
BLACKPINK
South Korea's biggest girl group returns to the US festival scene after making history as the first K-pop group to perform at Coachella in 2019. The four-member group made their debut in 2016 with dual title tracks “Whistle” and “Boombayah”, which marked their first chart-topping entries on Korea’s Gaon Digital Chart and Billboard’s World Digital Song Sales, respectively. BLACKPINK’s latest release, “The Album”, is the first and only album by a K-pop girl group to sell over one million copies.
EXO
One of the most commercially successful acts in K-pop, EXO made their breakthrough with their first album “XOXO” and hit single “Growl” in 2013. The album made EXO the first Korean one-million seller since 2001, and they went on to record sales of over one million copies for each of their following 5 Korean studio albums. The group’s success extended to its members’ solo activities: EXO’s main vocalist Baekhyun is K-pop’s only soloist to reach one million sales with multiple albums, and main dancer Lay was China’s best-selling artist in 2020.
NCT
NCT is K-pop's first act to experiment with an "unlimited" member lineup, adding new members and subunits constantly throughout their activities. NCT's current subunits include the rotational group NCT U, the Seoul-based NCT 127, the then-teenage-themed unit Dream, and their first overseas unit, WayV, based in China. While promoting as separate units, NCT has united as a 23-member group for 3 full-length albums.
Twice
The nine-member girl group is one of K-pop most prolific groups, having released 6 full albums and 11 EPs in both Korean and Japanese since their 2015 debut. Their extensive discography features hit single "Cheer Up", the best-selling song of 2016 in South Korea, which earned them great public recognition and a Song of the Year win at Korea's prestigious Melon Music Award, propelling them to domestic fame and commercial success. As of 2021, the group has surpassed over 10 million copies in total sales.
Seventeen
Often recognized as the best performance boy group of K-pop, Seventeen’s 13-member lineup have showcased their talents as three units specialized in vocal, hip-hop, and performance. The group won Best Male Dance Performance at the 2017 and 2018 Mnet Asian Music Awards, with choreographies for chart-topping singles “Don’t Wanna Cry” and “Oh My!”. They are also considered a "self-producing" idol group, with the members being actively involved in the songwriting, choreographing, and other aspects of their music and performances.

aespa
The youngest act of Street Sosh’s lineup, aespa made their debut in 2020 and have quickly risen to become the biggest girl group of K-pop’s fourth generation (2018–present). Their single “Next Level” was a viral hit in South Korea, earning them Song of the Year nominations and wins at major year-end award shows. aespa’s debut album, “Savage”, is currently the best-selling girl group debut album of all time in K-pop.
Red Velvet
The five-member group under SM Entertainment is one of the most critically-acclaimed K-pop acts, and is 2021’s fourth most streamed K-pop girl group on Spotify. Red Velvet's discography is defined by two concepts: the predominantly-pop "Red" side reflects a brighter, more vivid and energetic image, while their "Velvet" tracks feature softer, more elegant sounds and influences of R&B and ballad.
SUNMI
Sunmi debuted in 2007 as a member of Wonder Girls, best known for their triple hit songs “Tell Me”, “So Hot” and “Nobody”. The English version of hit single “Nobody” was the first track by a Korean artist to ever chart on the US Billboard Hot 100. Since the group’s disbandment in 2017, Sunmi has established herself as one of K-pop’s biggest solo artists. Her single “Gashina” was named 2017’s third best K-pop song by Billboard.
